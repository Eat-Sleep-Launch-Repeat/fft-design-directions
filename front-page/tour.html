Stop the Presses · Chapter OneThe Bellboy Who Still Announces Room Service

The Hotel Monte Vista opened on New Year's Day, 1927, and it has been collecting stories ever since. Ask the night clerk about the phantom bellboy: the crisp knock on the door, the muffled voice announcing room service, and no one in the hallway when the door swings open. Guests have reported him for decades. He is punctual, polite, and thoroughly dead.

Your guide will tell you which floor to ask about, which famous Hollywood guests refused to stay a second night, and why the bank downstairs kept its vault door locked long after the bank itself gave up. We start with the Monte Vista because it is the tour in miniature: a beautiful building, a warm lobby, and a history that never fully checked out.

From the Archive · Chapter TwoThe 1937 Cold Case That Still Haunts the Archives

Every town has one case it never solved and never forgot. Flagstaff's dates to 1937, and our co-founder Susan Johnson, the local author who literally wrote the book on haunted Flagstaff, has spent years in the archives with it. She shared the story of the Walkup family murders on national television when Travel Channel's The Dead Files came to town in 2022. On this tour, you get the long version, on the very blocks where it happened.

This is the difference between a local tour and a franchise. A chain guide reads a paragraph about 1937. Our guides can tell you what the newspapers printed that week, what the neighbors whispered, and which details never made the official record. History is not a script here. It is a neighbor.

City Desk · Chapter ThreeOne Easy Mile Under the Mountain Stars

The route covers one mile at a storyteller's pace: 75 minutes, mostly flat, all downtown, with stops timed so nobody is ever rushed past a good ghost. We run rain, snow or starlight, and at 7,000 feet the starlight usually wins. Bring a jacket, bring the family (we recommend 13 and up for the spookier stretches), and bring questions. Our guides love questions.

We gather at Wheeler Park, 212 W Aspen Ave, near City Hall, where your guide will be the one in period dress holding a lantern. Hard to miss. From there it is the Monte Vista, the Orpheum, the Weatherford, and a few corners we prefer to surprise you with. You will pass your own hotel and never look at it the same way again.
