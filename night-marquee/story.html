THE NOTORIOUS WALKUP FAMILY TRAGEDY

The heaviest story we tell, and the reason we tell every story the way we do: from the record, with respect.

Flagstaff, Arizona · By The Freaky Foot Tours research desk · Documented history · Summer 1937 · As featured on Travel Channel's The Dead Files (2022)

Every town keeps one story it lowers its voice for. In Flagstaff, it is the summer of 1937.

Flagstaff in 1937 was a small mountain town of lumber whistles and train schedules, the kind of place where the same few thousand people passed each other on the same few blocks every day. It was a town where news traveled by front porch faster than it traveled by press. And in that summer, the news that moved through those porches was the kind no town is ever prepared for.

Marie Walkup took the lives of her four children, and then her own. We will not dress that sentence up, and we will not dress it down. The papers of the day carried the story across the state, and the questions it raised, about what happened inside that home and why, were asked at every kitchen table in Flagstaff. Many of them have never been fully answered.

What remains today is the record: the newspaper accounts, the official documents, the recollections passed carefully down through Flagstaff families. That record is where our version of the story lives. Co-founder Susan Johnson, who wrote the book on haunted Flagstaff, spent years with this case, tracing what was printed, what was filed and what the town actually remembered, and separating each from the rumors that grew up around them.

"We do not perform this one. We carry it. It is told standing still, from the documents, and we let the quiet do the rest."

The Research Desk · Freaky Foot Tours

That research is why the story left Flagstaff. In 2022, Susan shared the Walkup case on Travel Channel's The Dead Files, bringing a national audience to a story this town had held privately for eighty five years. What the cameras found was what our guests find: not a haunted house attraction, but a documented tragedy that still shapes how Flagstaff remembers itself.

Downtown Flagstaff · Where the Story Is Told

On The Original Flagstaff Ghost Tour, the Walkup story comes near the end of the night, and it is told differently than every other stop. No theatrics. No jump scares. The guide sets down the lantern, gives the year, the names and the facts, and lets you stand for a moment in the same summer air. Guests regularly tell us it is the stop they think about on the drive home.

Why tell it at all? Because the alternative is worse. Left untold, a story like this curdles into rumor, and rumor is unkind to everyone in it. Told carefully, it becomes what it should be: the town remembering its own, out loud, once a night. That is the standard this story set for us, and every tale on every tour we run is held to it.
