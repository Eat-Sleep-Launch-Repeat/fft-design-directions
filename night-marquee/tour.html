Tonight's Feature

THE HOTELS THAT NEVER CHECKED OUT.

Flagstaff kept its 1920s hotels, its 1930s secrets and, by most accounts, several of its guests.

This is the tour that started it all in 2015: our original after-dark walk through downtown Flagstaff, written from local archives and led by guides who live here. You will stand under the neon at the Hotel Monte Vista, hear the phantom bellboy's knock, and walk the block where a 1930s cold case still has no ending.

✦The Hotel Monte Vista's phantom bellboy and its most famous permanent guests
✦The Orpheum Theater, a 1917 movie palace with an audience that never left
✦The Weatherford Hotel, Flagstaff's grand dame since 1900
✦A 1930s cold case told from the local files, not a national script
Stop · Hotel Monte Vista

01 · THE MONTE VISTA'S PERMANENT GUESTS

Since 1927 the Monte V has hosted movie stars, bootleggers and at least a few guests who never handed back their keys. Your guide will point at the exact window, name the room, and tell you what the night clerks still report. Our co-founder Susan Johnson literally wrote the book on haunted Flagstaff, and this building is why.

Reserve This Stop's Story
Stop · Orpheum Theater

02 · A MOVIE PALACE WITH AN ENCORE

The Orpheum has been showing pictures since 1917, and the staff will tell you some patrons take the phrase "final showing" loosely. Balcony seats that fold themselves down, projection booth footsteps, and a stage that has seen a century of last acts.

Stop · The Rail District

03 · THE 1930s COLD CASE

Every town has one story it lowers its voice for. Ours is a 1937 case that made statewide headlines and still has no clean ending. We tell it where it happened, from the archives, and we let you decide. This is the chapter that made the Travel Channel's The Dead Files come to Flagstaff in 2022.
