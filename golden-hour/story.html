The Notorious Walkup Family Tragedy

Summer 1937, Flagstaff. The heaviest story we tell on the Flagstaff ghost tour, researched from the record and told with respect.

📖 Researched by local author Susan Johnson 📺 Shared on Travel Channel's The Dead Files (2022)
🕯 By The Freaky Foot Tours research deskFlagstaff, Arizona · The events described took place in the summer of 1937 Flagstaff True Crime

Every town has one story it lowers its voice to tell. In Flagstaff, that story belongs to the summer of 1937, when Marie Walkup took the lives of her four children and then her own. Nearly ninety years later it remains the darkest chapter in the town's memory, and the heaviest stop on the Flagstaff ghost tour.

We want to be plain about what this story is and what it is not. It is not a campfire tale, and we do not treat it like one. A mother and four children died, in a real house, in a real town, in a summer their neighbors never forgot. When a story costs that much, we believe you owe it the discipline of the record: the documents, the newspaper accounts, the dates that can be checked.

That discipline is where Susan Johnson comes in. Susan, co-founder of Freaky Foot Tours and the local author who wrote the book on haunted Flagstaff, spent years researching the Walkup case, working from the archival record rather than the rumors that inevitably grow around a tragedy of this size. What we tell on the tour is what her research supports, and where the record goes quiet, so do we.

Freaky Foot Tours owner Nick Jones undergoes some paranormal testing and local author Susan Johnson shares the story of the 1937 Walkup family murders. Arizona Daily Sun · March 2022 · on Travel Channel's The Dead Files

In 2022, that research reached a national audience. Travel Channel's The Dead Files came to Flagstaff and filmed here, and Susan shared the Walkup story on the program. It was a strange kind of milestone: a case the town had spent decades keeping at the edge of conversation, told carefully, on the record, to millions.

On the tour, the Walkup story comes near the end of the night, and our guides handle it differently than every other stop. There are no theatrics, no gore and no jump scares. We tell you what is documented: what happened in the summer of 1937, how the town responded, and why Flagstaff still speaks of it quietly. Then we let the silence do what silence does.

Guests sometimes ask why we include it at all. The answer is that leaving it out felt worse. This is the town's history, and pretending otherwise would make the rest of our stories cheaper. Told from the record, with respect, it becomes what it should be: an act of remembering, for a mother and four children whose names deserve better than a whisper and a shudder.

✦ How we tell this one. The full account, with the documentation behind it, is shared in person on The Original Flagstaff Ghost Tour. We keep it factual and respectful, and guides are always glad to talk afterward if the story sits heavy.
